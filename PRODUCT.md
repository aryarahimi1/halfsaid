# Halfsaid — Product Context

register: product

## Product purpose

Halfsaid is a communication board for people who know exactly what they want to
say but have lost the bridge from thought to words. They tap a few broken
word-fragments; AI reconstructs the short sentence they already mean; it speaks
aloud, and for stroke survivors it speaks in their own voice (cloned from an old
recording). It runs free in a browser where the medical alternative is a
$5,000 to $15,000 device.

## Users

- **Primary:** stroke / aphasia survivors (cognition fully intact, only the
  thought-to-word bridge is broken) and minimally-verbal autistic people.
- **Secondary:** the caregiver or family member who sets it up and sits beside
  them.

Design for: low cognitive load, possible motor / coordination difficulty,
fatigue, no patience for hunting through grids, frequent re-tapping. Ages skew
old (stroke) or young (autistic teens). They are not "users testing an app",
they are people trying to be heard.

## Tone / voice

Calm, warm, dignified, plain. Never clinical, never childish, never "an AI
product." The interface should feel like it respects the person: unhurried,
legible, certain. No jargon ever reaches the user (the words "AAC", "profile",
"phrasebook" never appear on screen).

## The one moment

The unforgettable beat: a person taps three broken fragments and WATCHES them
become a whole sentence on screen, then hears it in their own voice. It proves
the AI is a bridge, not a replacement, and that they (not the machine) are the
author, the instant they reject a wrong guess in one tap. Everything else is
supporting cast for that moment.

## Anti-references (must NOT resemble)

- Generic AI-chat / SaaS dashboards: Inter, purple gradients, glassy cards,
  hero-metric blocks, neon "AI" sparkle, animated assistant orbs.
- Clinical medical-device UI: cold corporate blues, sterile grids, tiny dense
  controls.
- Childish "special-needs" apps: cartoon mascots, primary-color confetti,
  condescension.

## Strategic principles

- The human stays the author. AI proposes, the person confirms; a wrong guess is
  rejected in a single tap, visibly.
- Accessibility is the substance, not a checkbox: big targets, no timers, high
  contrast, full keyboard / switch reach, prefers-reduced-motion respected.
- Every surface a person can touch must work and feel certain.
- Offline-capable: the board must never go dark without signal.
